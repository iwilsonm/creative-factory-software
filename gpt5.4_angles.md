# Angles

These angles are written as structured creative briefs, not just topics.

Use each angle as a focused input for ad generation. The goal is to give the system one real buyer, one real problem pattern, one main objection, one scene, and one clear belief shift.

Important:
- Favor specificity over breadth.
- Favor one real person over a broad market.
- Favor believable relief over dramatic promises.
- Use MAHA and American-made framing as context when it strengthens the angle, not as filler.
- Scam framing should feel self-aware and trust-building, not cheap or theatrical.

## Broken Sleep / Wake Up at 2 to 4 AM
- **Status**: active
- **Priority**: highest
- **Frame**: symptom-first

### Core Buyer
Woman 50-70 who usually falls asleep without much trouble, but wakes up in the middle of the night and cannot get back to sleep.

### Symptom Pattern
Wakes between 2:00 and 4:00 AM most nights, sometimes to pee and sometimes for no clear reason. Lies awake for 1-3 hours. Feels dull, heavy, and less patient the next day.

### Failed Solutions
Magnesium, melatonin, herbal tea, no caffeine after noon, reading before bed, white noise, mouth tape, sleep hygiene advice.

### Current Belief
Thinks this may just be aging, hormones, stress, or how her body works now.

### Objection
Grounding sounds odd, too simple, or too woo-woo to help with a sleep problem this specific.

### Emotional State
Worn down, frustrated, quietly discouraged, tired of having another bad night without calling it insomnia.

### Scene to Center the Ad On
She checks the clock at 3:17 AM and already knows tomorrow will feel harder because of this.

### Desired Belief Shift
"This strange thing I almost ignored might actually help with the exact sleep problem I have."

### Tone
Calm, specific, skeptical-friendly, practical.

### Avoid
Generic insomnia language, mystical visuals, young models, aggressive cure claims.

# Notes for System Use

## Best categories to emphasize
1. Broken sleep
2. Mid-night waking
3. Morning stiffness
4. Back pain
5. Arthritis / joint discomfort
6. Nighttime painkiller reliance
7. Menopause-disrupted sleep
8. Skeptic conversion
9. American-made trust
10. MAHA-style sleep-environment cleanup

## What should carry the most weight
- Core Buyer
- Symptom Pattern
- Failed Solutions
- Objection
- Scene to Center the Ad On
- Desired Belief Shift

## Strong output signs
A strong ad from one of these angles should feel like:
- "That is me."
- "That is exactly what happens."
- "I thought this sounded fake too."
- "This brand gets why I would doubt this."
- "This feels specific enough to be worth a click."

## Weak output signs
A weak ad from one of these angles will sound like:
- broad wellness talk
- generic sleep claims
- generic inflammation claims
- lecture-style science
- fake patriotism
- abstract MAHA language with no symptom pattern
- scam framing that feels too theatrical
